## Abstract

We propose that reasoning in transformer language models is governed by a **thermodynamic‑geometric principle**: correct reasoning trajectories minimize a scalar free‑energy functional \(\mathcal{F}\) defined on the Fisher–Rao statistical manifold of token‑wise probability distributions. Hallucinations correspond to saddle points or maxima of \(\mathcal{F}\), and the onset of error is preceded by a **first‑order phase transition** in the Ricci curvature of the reasoning path. Four complementary observables—Ricci curvature, entropy production, topological persistence of attention graphs, and random‑matrix spectral statistics—each provide independent signatures of reasoning quality. Combined into \(\mathcal{F}\), they yield a unified early‑warning detector and a variational principle that could guide inference‑time corrections.

This whitepaper synthesizes the mathematical framework, the registered predictions, and the experimental roadmap. All reference code is provided in the `scripts/` directory of the repository, and all predictions are falsifiable with publicly available models (GPT‑2, LLaMA) and datasets (GSM8K, TriviaQA, HotpotQA).

## 1. Introduction

Large language models exhibit remarkable reasoning abilities, yet they also suffer from **hallucinations**—confident‑looking but factually incorrect outputs. Despite extensive empirical study, a **physical theory** of why hallucinations occur and how to detect them geometrically has been missing.

Starting from the observation that transformer activations form a **statistical manifold** (Amari, 2016), and that reasoning is a discrete‑time trajectory on that manifold, we have developed over 43 notes a framework that draws on:

- **Information geometry** (Fisher–Rao metric, geodesics)
- **Optimal transport** (Wasserstein distance)
- **Riemannian geometry** (coarse Ricci curvature)
- **Statistical physics** (entropy production, free energy)
- **Topological data analysis** (persistent homology)
- **Random matrix theory** (level‑spacing statistics)

The central claim is that **correct reasoning is a geodesic in the space of probability distributions** (Note #005), and that deviations from geodesicity—measured by Ricci curvature collapse, entropy spikes, topological fragmentation, and spectral delocalization—precede and predict hallucination.

## 2. The Manifold of Thought

Let \( \mathcal{V} \) be the vocabulary of size \( V \). Each token generated by a language model corresponds to a probability distribution \( p \in \Delta^{V-1} \) (the simplex). The **Fisher–Rao metric** on the simplex is:

\[
g_{ij}(p) = \sum_{k} \frac{1}{p_k} \frac{\partial p_k}{\partial \theta_i} \frac{\partial p_k}{\partial \theta_j},
\]

where \( \theta \) are coordinates (e.g., logits). The geodesic distance between two distributions \( p, q \) is the **Hellinger distance**:

\[
d_H(p, q) = \left( \sum_{k} (\sqrt{p_k} - \sqrt{q_k})^2 \right)^{1/2}.
\]

A reasoning trajectory is a sequence \( \{p_t\}_{t=1}^T \) of token distributions. The **cumulative entropy production** is the KL divergence between consecutive steps:

\[
\Delta S(t) = D_{\text{KL}}(p_{t+1} \| p_t).
\]

**Prediction (Note #011):** Entropy production is approximately constant along correct reasoning and spikes at the onset of hallucination.

## 3. Curvature Collapse

The **coarse (Ollivier) Ricci curvature** along the edge between \( p_t \) and \( p_{t+1} \) is:

\[
\kappa(t) = 1 - \frac{W_1(p_t, p_{t+1})}{d_H(p_t, p_{t+1})},
\]

where \( W_1 \) is the 1‑Wasserstein distance. Positive curvature indicates contraction of the probability metric (coherent reasoning). Negative curvature signals divergence of the reasoning path.

**Prediction (Note #042, H1):** Correct trajectories have \( \kappa(t) > 0 \) for all \( t \); hallucinated trajectories have at least one \( \kappa(t) < 0 \), and the first negative step precedes the semantic error.

**Phase transition:** The cumulative curvature \( K(t) = \sum_{i=1}^t \kappa(i) \) shows a discontinuous drop at hallucination onset, analogous to a first‑order phase transition in statistical mechanics.

## 4. Topological Signatures of Attention

The attention matrix \( A^{(\ell)} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n} \) at layer \( \ell \) can be thresholded to form a weighted adjacency graph. The **0‑dimensional persistent homology** of this graph measures the number of connected components as a function of threshold.

**Prediction (Note #041):** Multi‑step reasoning tasks produce significantly more persistent components than single‑step retrieval tasks, and the number scales with the logarithm of required reasoning steps.

## 5. Spectral Regularization (Random Matrix Theory)

The eigenvalue spectrum of the attention matrix (after normalizing by its mean) follows a **Wigner‑like law** for random noise but deviates for structured reasoning. The variance of the unfolded nearest‑neighbor spacing, \( \text{Var}(s) \), is:

- ≈ 0.27 (Wigner surmise) for random attention,
- > 0.5 for structured reasoning,
- and spikes before hallucination.

**Prediction (Note #037, Note #043):** Incorporating \( \text{Var}(s) \) as a regularization term improves the discriminative power of the free energy.

## 6. Unified Free Energy of Reasoning (FER)

We define:

\[
\mathcal{F}[ \{p_t\}, \{A^{(\ell)}\} ] = -\sum_{t=1}^{T-1} \kappa(t) \;+\; \lambda_1 \sum_{t=1}^{T-1} \Delta S(t) \;+\; \lambda_2 \, \tau_{\text{pers}} \;+\; \lambda_3 \, \text{Var}(s),
\]

where:
- \( \kappa(t) \) = coarse Ricci curvature,
- \( \Delta S(t) \) = KL divergence,
- \( \tau_{\text{pers}} \) = sum of persistence lengths of 0‑D features in attention,
- \( \text{Var}(s) \) = variance of level spacings of attention eigenvalues.

The probability of a trajectory being correct follows a Boltzmann law:

\[
p(\text{correct}) \propto \exp(-\beta \mathcal{F}),
\]

with a single temperature \( \beta \) (estimated from data).

**Prediction (Note #043):** AUC > 0.80 on GPT‑2 small with GSM8K, and AUC > 0.99 on synthetic benchmarks.

## 8. Implications and Open Questions

If confirmed, this framework provides:

1. **A unified theory** linking geometry, thermodynamics, and topology of reasoning.
2. **A real‑time hallucination detector** requiring only the forward pass logits and attention matrices (no external knowledge).
3. **A variational principle** that could guide fine‑tuning or inference‑time correction (e.g., via differentiable optimization of \( \mathcal{F} \)).

**Open questions:**
- Does the functional \( \mathcal{F} \) generalize across model scales (GPT‑2 small → LLaMA‑7B)?
- Is the temperature \( \beta \) universal?
- Can \( \mathcal{F} \) be minimized in logit space to correct errors at inference time?
- Are there additional terms (e.g., Ricci “jerk”, higher‑order persistence)?

These are left for future notes.
