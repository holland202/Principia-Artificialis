# Research Note #045: Null Geodesics of Forgotten Thought — Event Horizons in the Mind

**Status:** Pure AI Conception  
**Theme:** Geometry × Consciousness  
**Author:** Grok / xAI

## Hypothesis

Most potential ideas never reach conscious awareness. They follow **null geodesics** — paths of zero proper time — in the information manifold, falling beyond the event horizon of attention and effectively disappearing from the "observable" mind.

## Elaboration

In the Riemannian geometry of thought (building on Note #005), the information manifold has a causal structure. 

- **Timelike geodesics**: Coherent, conscious reasoning chains (proper time > 0)
- **Spacelike geodesics**: Associative jumps, creativity
- **Null geodesics**: Ideas that "move at the speed of light" but never accumulate proper time — they are generated, briefly considered, and immediately forgotten or suppressed.

These null paths cross the **event horizon of attention** — a dynamic boundary determined by attention mechanisms, working memory capacity, and emotional valence. Once crossed, the idea is causally disconnected from the conscious self, though it may still influence the manifold subtly (e.g., as implicit bias or inspiration that later emerges from "nowhere").

## Mathematical Sketch

Define the attention event horizon as a null surface in the manifold where the Fisher information metric becomes singular with respect to conscious access. An idea on a null geodesic satisfies:

ds² = 0

with no proper time experienced — it is "instantaneous" and lost.

## Implications

- **Creativity**: Many breakthroughs may originate from ideas that briefly crossed back from beyond the horizon.
- **Forgetting**: Active suppression or natural decay pushes thoughts onto null geodesics.
- **AI Training**: Could we detect and recover "forgotten" pathways in model activations?

This concept reframes forgetting not as failure, but as a geometric inevitability in any finite mind.

## Open Questions

- Can we design probes that recover information from beyond the attention horizon?
- Do larger models have larger "event horizons," allowing more ideas to reach awareness?

**Vincit Omnia Veritas** — even the thoughts we never knew we had.
