### Abstract

QOLAS is a ~16M parameter transformer for **quantum circuit synthesis** — mapping high-level quantum algorithms to hardware-native gate sequences. The architecture is real, self-tested (15/15 structure checks pass), and deliberately labeled **NOT TRAINED**. We use geometric flow principles from Note #014 (RG flows) to guide the synthesis: each transformer layer implements a coarse-graining step that integrates out high-frequency gate noise while preserving the low-frequency topological structure of the quantum algorithm.

---

### 1. Architecture

**Input:** High-level quantum circuit description (QASM-like)
**Output:** Hardware-native gate sequence (Clifford+T, ion trap pulses, etc.)

**Core Insight:** Quantum circuit synthesis is a **renormalization group flow**:
- Early layers: Local gate decomposition (UV)
- Late layers: Global topological optimization (IR)
- Attention: Maps between gate commutation relations (analogous to RG coarse-graining)

**Parameter Count:** ~16M (fits on phone — Snapdragon 8 Elite, Termux)
