## Abstract

Current AI systems excel at statistical prediction yet lack a unified mathematical description of cognition itself. Memory, reasoning, uncertainty, exploration, and learning are typically implemented as separate engineering components rather than manifestations of a common mathematical structure.

This work proposes a unified framework in which cognition is modeled as the evolution of a geometric state over an **information manifold**. Drawing on differential geometry, information geometry, topology, stochastic processes, dynamical systems, and quantum information theory, we define a family of operators that describe memory formation, reasoning trajectories, curiosity-driven exploration, and synthetic knowledge generation.

Several mathematical objects introduced here — the **Memory Tension Tensor**, **Reasoning Curvature Tensor**, **Cognitive Coherence Operator**, and **Synthetic Knowledge Potential** — are proposed constructs intended to generate testable hypotheses. Their theoretical properties and empirical utility remain to be established through experimentation.
