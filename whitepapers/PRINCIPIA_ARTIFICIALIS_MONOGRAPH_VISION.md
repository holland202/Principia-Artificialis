## Abstract

Current AI systems excel at statistical prediction yet lack a unified mathematical description of cognition itself. Memory, reasoning, uncertainty, exploration, and learning are treated as separate engineering components rather than manifestations of a common underlying structure.

This work proposes a unified geometric and topological framework in which cognition is modeled as the evolution of a state over an **information manifold**. Drawing from differential geometry, information geometry, topology, quantum information, and dynamical systems, we introduce a family of new mathematical objects and operators.

Key proposed constructs include the **Memory Tension Tensor**, **Reasoning Curvature Tensor**, **Cognitive Coherence Operator**, **Scar Curvature Tensor**, **Epistemic Stress Tensor**, and **Synthetic Knowledge Potential**. These are presented as hypothesis-generating tools, not established results. Their properties and utility will be validated through formal proofs, reference implementations, and empirical experiments.
